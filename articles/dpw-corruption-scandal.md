The San Francisco Department of Public Works corruption scandal was a
federal bribery and honest-services-fraud case that became public with the
January 2020 arrest of Mohammed Nuru, then director of SF Public Works.[^ml]

In August 2022, Nuru was sentenced to 84 months in federal prison after
pleading guilty to accepting bribes from city contractors.[^kqed]
